# Private Research Policy

The private research companion is for one subscriber's personal research workflow. It should
make subscribed research easier to organize and understand, but it must respect copyright,
subscription terms, and financial-advice boundaries.

## Personal-Use Scope

Allowed uses:

- ingest documents the user is already entitled to access;
- summarize and compare research locally for that user;
- preserve source attribution and document history;
- produce private notes, search results, and digests.

Out of scope:

- sharing paid content with other users;
- publishing private summaries as a public feed;
- copying or exporting full paid reports for redistribution;
- using private subscription text in the public daily market brief;
- providing personalized financial advice.

## Subscription And Copyright Caution

A valid login does not automatically permit automated scraping, bulk downloading, storage,
copying, summarization, or redistribution. Before any automation is added, the user must confirm
that the subscription terms permit the exact access pattern.

Preferred ingestion paths are user-driven:

- upload a PDF;
- select a local downloaded file;
- forward or save an email;
- paste a manual note;
- import a permitted subscriber export.

## Logged-In Automation

Logged-in automation is disabled by default. It must remain unavailable unless a later connector
requires explicit user confirmation that subscription terms permit it.

No logged-in Under the Radar connector or scraper exists in the current step.

## Source Attribution

Every private document should retain:

- source name;
- document title;
- access method;
- URL when available;
- author when available;
- publication timestamp when available;
- retrieval/import timestamp;
- subscription and licence notes.

## Advice Boundary

Private summaries may extract factual report content, recommendation labels, risks, valuation
assumptions, catalysts, and watch items from a source document. They must not tell the user what
to buy, sell, hold, rebalance, or trade based on the user's personal circumstances.
